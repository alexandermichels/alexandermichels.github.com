Particle Swarm Optimization

Particle Swarm Optimization allows particles to explore a space influenced by its current velocity (\(~\overrightarrow{v_{t}}~\)), the best position it has found (\(~\overrightarrow{p_{b}}~\)), and the best position its neighbors have found (\(~\overrightarrow{g_{b}}~\)):

The math:

velocity update:
\(\overrightarrow{v}_{t+1} = k(\overrightarrow{v}_{t}+c \beta_{1} (\overrightarrow{p}_{b}-\overrightarrow{p}_{t})+s \beta_{2} (\overrightarrow{g}_{b}-\overrightarrow{p}_{t}))\)

position update:
\(\overrightarrow{p}_{t+1} = \overrightarrow{p}_{t} + \overrightarrow{v}_{t+1}\)

the "constriction factor":
where \(k = \frac{2}{\Vert 2-\phi-\sqrt{\phi^{2}-4\phi}\Vert}, \: \: \phi=c + s, \phi > 4\)

"velocity clamping":
\(\overrightarrow{v_{t}}= \overrightarrow{v}_{t}\cdot \frac{\texttt{vmax}}{\Vert\overrightarrow{v}_{t}\Vert}\), if \(\Vert\overrightarrow{v}_{t}\Vert> \texttt{vmax}\)

\(\beta_{1},\beta_{2}\) are uniform i.i.d. random variables
\(c,s\) are cognitive and social constants resp.
\(\overrightarrow{p}_{t},\overrightarrow{v}_{t}\) are position and velocity at time step

Why are particles shooting off the screen?

"Letting the particles fly" outside of the region of interest reduces bias to the center of the region.

How does maximum velocity effect PSO?

A lower vmax allows higher granularity search, but you might not explore as much of the region

How does the agent-based model work?


To test the efficacy of PSO for spatially-explicit agent-based models, we chose to use a model designed for studying the transmission of influenza. It uses an SEIR model which stands for

  • Susceptible
  • Exposed
  • Infectious
  • Recovered
