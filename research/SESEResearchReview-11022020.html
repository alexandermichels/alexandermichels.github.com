Particle Swarm Optimization

Particle Swarm Optimization allows particles to explore a space influenced by its current velocity (\(~\overrightarrow{v_{t}}~\)), the best position it has found (\(~\overrightarrow{p_{b}}~\)), and the best position its neighbors have found (\(~\overrightarrow{g_{b}}~\)):

\(\overrightarrow{v}_{t+1} = k(\overrightarrow{v}_{t}+c \beta_{1} (\overrightarrow{p}_{b}-\overrightarrow{p}_{t})+s \beta_{2} (\overrightarrow{g}_{b}-\overrightarrow{p}_{t}))\)      <-- this is velocity update

\(\overrightarrow{p}_{t+1} = \overrightarrow{p}_{t} + \overrightarrow{v}_{t+1}\)      <-- this is position update

where \(k = \frac{2}{\Vert 2-\phi-\sqrt{\phi^{2}-4\phi}\Vert}, \: \: \phi=c + s, \phi > 4\)      <-- this is the "constriction factor"

\(\overrightarrow{v_{t}}= \overrightarrow{v}_{t}\cdot \frac{\texttt{vmax}}{\Vert\overrightarrow{v}_{t}\Vert}\), if \(\Vert\overrightarrow{v}_{t}\Vert> \texttt{vmax}\)      <-- this is "velocity clamping"

\(\beta_{1},\beta_{2}\) are uniform i.i.d. random variables
\(c,s\) are cognitive and social constants resp.
\(\overrightarrow{p}_{t},\overrightarrow{v}_{t}\) are position and velocity at time step

  • Exposed
