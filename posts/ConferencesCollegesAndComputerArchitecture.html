Conferences, Colleges, and Computer Architecture
Alexander Michels

I recently had the pleasure of giving a talk at the 2019 Joint Mathematics Meeting in Baltimore, MD. It was an incredible opportunity to present mine and Himanshu's work on computational fact-checking. We were invited to give a talk during the AMS Contributed Paper Session on Operations Research, Mathematical Programming, Optimization, Game Theory, Economics, and Mathematics in the Social and Behavioral Sciences and also to participate in the Undergraduate Poster Session. Below you can check out the slides from my presentation (unfortunately Himanshu wasn't able to fly out) and check out our poster from the conference.

The conference was not only an incredible opportunity to skip my first week of classes for the Spring 2019 semester, it was a lot of fun to hear about the amazing work happening in math around the world and make some great connections. I can't thank Westminster's Drinko Center and UCLA's Institute for Pure and Applied Mathematics enough for flying me out to Baltimore, paying for my registration, and covering my stay for the week. Besides getting to have fun at the Batlimore Convention Center, it was a great opportunity to see my incredible coworkers from RIPS again, many of whom were also at JMM presenting. We all got together and had a small reunion dinner Friday night which was a lot of fun!

I am incredibly lucky to be applying in a time where these programs are springing up and growing and I can't thank my teachers, professors, parents, and friends enough for getting me to where I am.
